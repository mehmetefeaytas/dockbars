Everything, one flick away

Built for people who live in the Dock.

🫙

Unlimited stashes

Group apps and files into named stashes — Work, Dev, Games. Switch with ⌘1–9.

⌨️

Search & keyboard

Start typing to fuzzy-search across every stash. Arrow keys and Return, no mouse needed.

🧩

More than apps

Files, folders, websites, Shortcuts, shell scripts and text snippets — each opens the right way.

🪟

Liquid Glass

A translucent panel that feels native on macOS 26, with System, Light and Dark themes.

🖥️

Multi-monitor

The pocket opens on the screen your pointer is on, beside that display's Dock.

⚡

Featherweight

A single cached rect-test on pointer move. Idle CPU near zero, memory in tens of MB.

📋

Widgets & clipboard

Clock, battery and recent Downloads at a glance, plus an optional local clipboard history.

🚀

Automation

Open a stash from anywhere with ⌥Space, the dockbars:// URL scheme, or the CLI.

🔒

Yours alone

Everything stays on your Mac. No accounts, no network, no analytics — by design.

Three moves

It works the way the Dock already does.

  1. 1

    Reach the edge

    Move your pointer to the corner beside the Dock. A thin trigger strip is all it takes.

  2. 2

    The pocket slides out

    A glass panel springs open without stealing focus from whatever you were doing.

  3. 3

    Open, or move on

    Click to launch, drag to add or remove, or just glide away — it closes on its own.

Privacy is the default

Local-first, not local-when-convenient.

Dockbars has no network code. Your stashes, clipboard history and statistics never leave your Mac — there is nothing to opt out of, because there is nothing sending anything.
