Three moves

It works the way the Dock already does.

  1. 1

    Reach the edge

    Move your pointer to the corner beside the Dock. A thin trigger strip is all it takes.

  2. 2

    The pocket slides out

    A glass panel springs open without stealing focus from whatever you were doing.

  3. 3

    Open, or move on

    Click to launch, drag to add or remove, or just glide away — it closes on its own.

Questions

Good to know.

Does Dockbars replace the Dock?

No. It sits beside your Dock as a hidden pocket and opens on hover — the Dock stays exactly as it is.

Why does it need Accessibility access?

To sense the pointer reaching the Dock edge and to read the Dock's position. It requests no network access, ever.

Does any data leave my Mac?

No. There is no network code at all — stashes, clipboard history and statistics are stored locally only.

Which Macs are supported?

macOS 15 Sequoia or later, on both Apple Silicon and Intel.

Privacy is the default

Local-first, not local-when-convenient.

Dockbars has no network code. Your stashes, clipboard history and statistics never leave your Mac — there is nothing to opt out of, because there is nothing sending anything.
